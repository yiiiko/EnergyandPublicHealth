Nitrogen Oxide (NOx)

Nitrogen oxides (NOx) are gaseous air pollutants which include nitrogen monoxide (NO) and nitrogen dioxide (NO2 ).

Emission Sources

These gases are produced during the combustion (burning) of fossil fuels. Combustion of coal and natural gas in power plants are a major source of NOx. The transportation sector is also a major source for NOx as a result of combustion of gasoline and diesel in the engines of cars, trucks and buses. NO2 along with volatile organic compounds (like gasoline) react in the atmosphere to form ozone (O3), another air pollutant.

Ecosystem Impact

NOx contribute to acid deposition (rain and snow). Acid deposition is responsible for damaging pristine forests and aquatic ecosystems, including killing of trees and fish and impacting soil quality. NOx emissions also contribute to eutrophication.

Regulations

In the U.S. the 1970s Clean Air Act (CAA) and subsequent amendments regulate emissions of NOx. As a result of regulations, levels of this air pollutant have on average decreased.
