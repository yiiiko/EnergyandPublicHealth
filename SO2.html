Sulfur Dioxide (SO2)

Sulfur dioxide (SO2) is a gaseous air pollutant.

Emission Sources

It is produced during the combustion (burning) of fossil fuels. Combustion of coal in power plants is the primary source of SO2. Diesel engines in trucks and buses are also sources of SO2.

Health Impact

“Sulfur dioxide causes a range of harmful effects on the lungs:

  • Wheezing, shortness of breath and chest tightness and other problems, especially during exercise or physical activity.
  • Continued exposure at high levels increases respiratory symptoms and reduces the ability of the lungs to function.
  • Short exposures to peak levels of SO2 in the air can make it difficult for people with asthma to breathe when they are active outdoors.
  • Rapid breathing during exercise helps SO2 reach the lower respiratory tract, as does breathing through the mouth.
  • Increased risk of hospital admissions or emergency room visits, especially among children, older adults and people with asthma.”

Ecosystem Impact

SO2 contributes to acid deposition (rain and snow). Acid deposition is responsible for damaging pristine forests and aquatic ecosystems, including killing of trees and fish and impacting soil quality.

Regulations

In the U.S. the 1970s Clean Air Act (CAA) and subsequent amendments regulate emissions of SO2. As a result of regulations, levels of this air pollutant have on average decreased.
